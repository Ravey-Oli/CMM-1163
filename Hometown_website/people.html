People and Citzens

Today Florence is home to 370,705 residents, with the surrounding urban and metropolitan area being estimated to be 696,767 residents.

The population being significantly smaller in the past, in 1200 the population was 50,000 then by 1300 that grew to 120,000, after the black death plague of 1348 demographics of the 1500s and 1600s population declined to 70,000.

In the modern day most residents speak the Florentine dialect, parent language of the modern Italian language, vocabulary and pronunciation is nearly identical with some minor variations on certain words.

Florence is the birthplace of many famous iconic artists and significant historical people such as, Leonardo Da Vinci, Michelangelo, Rapheal, Donatello and Dante being headline names.

Other notable figures include political theorist Machiavelli, astronomer Galileo and humanitarian Florence Nightingale, all of whom called it home.
