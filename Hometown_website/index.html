The cradle of the renaissance, Florence the capital of the Tuscany region in Italy, once the most influential city of the era, during its golden age of the Italian renaissance.

Formerly known as the Duchy of Florence was home to the prestigious noble house, the “Medici” family during the fabled renaissance era of innovation and cultural revolution.

The origins are Roman, established in the first century by Julias Ceaser for his veteran legionnaire soldiers, originally named “Florentia” and built as a military outpost.

Becoming part of the Kingdom of United Italy in 1865, taking the capital spot from Turin before giving it up to Rome six years later.

Being listed a UNESCO world heritage site in 1982 for it's extraordinary 600 years of artistic activity.”“ The city is a harmonious blend of the modern and classic architecture, each building is a work of art, harboring even more creative art inside.

Explore mediterranean culture at its most grand, experience authentic Italian cuisine and see the masterworks of the renaissance's best artists.
